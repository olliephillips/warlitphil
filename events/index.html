Events

From March 2022, events will again be held live at Friars Green Methodist Church hall. 

Our series of lectures from Speakers eminent in their respective fields, aim to provide stimulating talks and discussions on matters of science, art, history, politics, music, literature etc

03

Oct

Anthotypes and Chlorophyll printing

Nettie Edwards explains two fascinating printing techniques. In the first, dye is extracted from plant matter and used to coat paper, the Anthotype. The subject is placed on top of the paper and both are exposed to sunlight. The Chlorophyll process is simpler: a transparency is placed on top of a leaf which is then left out in the sun.

 Nettie Edwards
 03/10/2022, 7:30PM

Ruskin and his legacies

Author and critic Dinah Birch, Professor of English Literature at Liverpool University, will talk about the writer, critic and reformer John Ruskin, what makes his work distinctive and why his legacies seem so valuable today.

 Dinah Birch
 07/11/2022, 7:30PM

The War of Nerves

Martin Sixsmith, writer and radio/TV presenter will talk about his recent book which looks inside the Cold War mind. The psychology behind an age of nuclear brinkmanship terrified a generation. This was the era of mutually assured destruction, when military leaders and statesmen prepared for a global nuclear war in which tens of millions of people would die. Are we going back to that?

 Martin Sixsmith
 05/12/2022, 7:30PM

The Future of Humanism

Humanists are people who shape their own lives in the here and now, because they believe it is the only life we have. They make sense of the world through logic, reason and evidence. Andrew will talk about the future of the movement which can be traced back over 2,500 years.

 Andrew Copson
